The Evidence: Where Story Meets Map

The frameworks on this site—from the Glass Box to the 26 Laws—were not created in a vacuum. They were extracted from the wreckage of a 43-year case study detailed in the memoir, From the Storm to the Fire. This page shows the essential link between the theory and the lived terrain.

The Memoir: The Lived Terrain

This site's frameworks are not abstract theory; they are the architectural blueprints extracted directly from a 43-year case study of Complex Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (CPTSD). The memoir, From the Storm to the Fire: 26 Laws of Survival, provides the raw, chronological evidence of how the survival system adapts when danger is the status quo.

Mapping the Framework to the Life

1. The Glass Box Blueprint (The Beginning)

Theory: The neurological mechanism for chronic hypervigilance and emotional dysregulation established in childhood.

Evidence in Memoir: Details the *Storm* period of childhood—the continuous state of high-alert, the impossible double binds, and the neurological hijacking that wired the narrator for danger and detachment. This is where the foundation of the CPTSD survival architecture is laid.

Explore the Glass Box

2. The 26 Laws (The Mechanics)

Theory: The set of psychological and behavioral adaptations developed in adolescence and adulthood to navigate a world that still feels hostile. These Laws serve as a functional operating system for trauma survival.

Evidence in Memoir: Details the *Fire* period—the decades-long, often self-destructive, attempt to find safety and agency using these internal, unwritten rules. Each Law is illustrated with pivotal life events, demonstrating its protective purpose and its cost.

View the 26 Laws

3. The Glossary (The Language)

Theory: Translating clinical, often abstract, terminology (like Emotional Dysregulation and Hypervigilance) into concrete, lived experience.

Evidence in Memoir: The narrative is built upon the author's internal lexicon for survival. The Glossary links the academic terms to the protagonist's specific functional behaviors, making complex concepts immediately relatable and validating.
