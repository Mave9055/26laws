Finding Professional Help

What to Look For in a Trauma Therapist

The most important thing is feeling like you can learn to trust your therapist. Trust your gut.

A good trauma therapist will:

  • Have understanding of trauma and CPTSD specifically
  • Be trained in trauma modalities (EMDR, CBT, DBT, etc.)
  • Hold firm boundaries
  • Engage in self-care outside their work
  • Hold hope for healing
  • Be strength-based and flexible to adapt treatment to your needs

Key Areas of Expertise to Seek

  • Complex Trauma Specific experience with CPTSD, not just PTSD
  • Relational Trauma Understanding of interpersonal and attachment wounds
  • Developmental Trauma Knowledge of how childhood trauma impacts adult functioning
  • Attachment Theory Framework for understanding relational patterns
  • Dissociation Experience working with dissociative symptoms

Evidence-Based Treatment Modalities

These are therapeutic approaches with research support for treating PTSD and CPTSD:

  • EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) - Uses bilateral stimulation to process traumatic memories
  • CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) - Addresses thought patterns and beliefs shaped by trauma
  • DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy) - Focuses on emotion regulation and distress tolerance skills
  • CPT (Cognitive Processing Therapy) - Specifically designed for trauma, addresses stuck points
  • PE (Prolonged Exposure) - Gradual, controlled exposure to trauma memories and reminders
  • Somatic Experiencing - Body-based trauma processing approach

Important Disclaimers

This Site Provides Peer Education, Not Clinical Treatment

I am not a therapist, psychologist, psychiatrist, or medical professional. I'm someone with lived experience of Complex PTSD who has spent years studying the condition and developing frameworks to understand it.

The content on this site is educational in nature. It's meant to help you understand CPTSD, recognize patterns, and have language for your experiences. It is NOT:

  • A substitute for professional mental health care
  • Medical or psychological advice
  • A diagnostic tool
  • Treatment for mental health conditions

If you're struggling with trauma symptoms, please seek care from a qualified, trauma-informed mental health professional.

Privacy & Safety Note

If you're in an unsafe situation (domestic violence, stalking, etc.), be aware that your internet activity may be monitored. Use a safe device and consider clearing your browser history. The National Domestic Violence Hotline has a quick exit button on their website.

Additional Educational Resources

Recommended Books

  • "The Body Keeps the Score" by Bessel van der Kolk - Essential reading on trauma's impact on the body
  • "Complex PTSD: From Surviving to Thriving" by Pete Walker - Comprehensive guide written by therapist with CPTSD
  • "Trauma and Recovery" by Judith Herman - Foundational work that first defined Complex PTSD
  • "What My Bones Know" by Stephanie Foo - Powerful memoir of CPTSD recovery

Online Communities (Use with Caution)

Online support can be valuable, but it's important to maintain boundaries and be aware that not all advice will be healthy or applicable to your situation.

  • r/CPTSD on Reddit - Large community, use moderation filters
  • CPTSD Foundation - Educational resources and support
  • National Center for PTSD - Research-based information

A Note on Online Communities

Online support spaces can be helpful, but they can also become echo chambers or inadvertently reinforce unhealthy patterns. They're not substitutes for professional care. If you notice a community is making you feel worse, it's okay to step back.

About This Site

The Wounded Blueprint is a peer education platform created to provide clear, direct information about Complex PTSD without the clinical distance or toxic positivity that dominates most trauma resources.

The frameworks and materials here come from years of lived experience, research, and the process of making sense of my own trauma architecture. I share them because understanding the blueprint was the first critical step in my own healing.

If these materials help you map your own wreckage, that's what they're for.
