Glossary of Terms

Defining the vocabulary of complex trauma, systemic survival, and the 26 Laws.

A

Adaptive Personality

/əˈdaptiv ˌpərsəˈnalitē/

A survival-based identity construct developed in response to prolonged trauma. Unlike a core personality, the adaptive personality prioritizes safety, camouflage, and the needs of the environment over authentic self-expression.

Note: Often referred to in the course as "The Mask."

Agency

/ˈājənsē/

The capacity of an individual to act independently and make free choices. In the context of C-PTSD, agency is often systematically dismantled by the traumatic environment.

T

Temporal Dysregulation

/ˈtemp(ə)rəl ˌdisˌreɡyəˈlāSHən/

The clinical phenomenon where the past (trauma) intrudes upon the present with the same sensory intensity as when it first occurred, making it difficult for the individual to perceive a distinct "future."

See: Law 11 - The Now-Future Paradox.
